Day Trip — Colombo to Galle

Start early · Use public transport · Return by evening

6:00 AMDepart

🟢 Depart — Colombo Fort Railway Station

Head to Colombo Fort Station early and board the 06:05 or 06:25 coastal train to Galle. Grab a window seat on the right side for the best ocean views. The journey hugs the Indian Ocean shoreline for much of the route — one of Asia's most scenic short rail trips.

🚂 Coastal Train ~2.5 hrs LKR 100–600 Right-side seat for views
8:30 AMMorning

🏁 Arrive — Galle Railway Station

Disembark at Galle Station and walk roughly 10–15 minutes along the waterfront road towards the iconic fort walls. Stop for a quick breakfast at a local café near the station before entering the fort.

🚶 10 min walk to fort ☕ Breakfast stop
9:00 AM~2 hrs

🏰 Galle Dutch Fort — Exploration

Spend the cooler morning hours exploring the UNESCO-listed fort. Walk the ancient sea-facing ramparts, visit the Galle Lighthouse (oldest in Sri Lanka), pop into the Dutch Reformed Church (1755), and browse the fascinating National Maritime Museum. The streets inside are wonderfully photogenic.

🏰 Fort Ramparts 🔦 Galle Lighthouse 🏛️ Dutch Church ~2 hrs Free entry (most areas)
11:00 AM~1.5 hrs

🛕 Religious Sites & Pedlar Street

Explore the fort's religious diversity — visit the serene Sri Sudharmalaya Buddhist Temple with its intricate murals, and the Meeran Jumma Mosque with its beautiful Moorish architecture. Then stroll Pedlar Street, lined with boutique shops, galleries, gem stores, and local craft sellers.

🛕 Buddhist Temple 🕌 Mosque 🛍️ Pedlar Street ~1.5 hrs
12:30 PM~1.5 hrs

🍛 Lunch — Fort Café District

Choose from the many excellent restaurants inside and just outside the fort. For traditional Sri Lankan flavours, try a rice & curry meal at one of the local spots near the market. For a scenic lunch, rooftop cafés along the ramparts offer ocean views with fresh seafood.

🍛 Local Rice & Curry 🦞 Fresh Seafood Budget ~LKR 600–1,200 ~1.5 hrs
2:00 PM~2.5 hrs

🏖️ Unawatuna Beach

Take a short tuk-tuk (5 min, ~LKR 200–300) or walk to beautiful Unawatuna Beach for a relaxing afternoon. The sheltered bay has calm, clear water ideal for swimming. You can rent snorkelling gear to explore the coral reefs, or simply relax under a palm tree with a cool drink.

🏖️ Swim & Snorkel 🛺 5 min tuk-tuk ~2.5 hrs Bring sunscreen
4:30 PM~1 hr

🌅 Golden Hour — Fort Ramparts

Return to the fort for the golden hour. The western-facing ramparts offer a spectacular sunset view over the Indian Ocean — arguably one of the most beautiful sunsets in Sri Lanka. The warm light on the old colonial architecture is magical for photography.

📷 Golden Hour 🌅 Sunset Views ~1 hr Bring camera
6:00 PMReturn

🏁 Return to Colombo

Make your way to Galle Station (10 min walk) and board the evening train back to Colombo. The return journey at dusk offers a completely different but equally beautiful coastal experience. Last trains typically depart around 18:30–20:00 — check current schedules beforehand.

🚂 Train or 🚌 Bus ~2.5 hrs return ⚠ Check schedules

🚂 Book Train Early

The scenic coastal train is popular. Book 2nd or 3rd class early morning for the best experience.

👟 Wear Comfortable Shoes

Cobblestone streets inside the fort require comfortable walking shoes. Avoid flip-flops for long walks.

🌞 Beat the Heat

Start fort sightseeing early morning when it's cooler. Head to the beach in the afternoon.

📱 Download Offline Maps

Download Galle offline maps before you go. Mobile data can be patchy near coastal areas.

Also Consider

Day Trips from Galle

🐋 Mirissa (24 km)

Add a morning whale-watching trip from Mirissa before heading to Galle. Boats leave at dawn and return by 10 AM — timing pairs perfectly with the afternoon fort visit.

🐢 Kosgoda (20 km)

Stop at the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery on the way to or from Galle. Best visited at dusk when turtle activity is highest. Easily accessible from the main coastal road.

🌿 Kanneliya (23 km)

For nature lovers, the Kanneliya Rainforest is a half-day excursion from Galle. Hire a local guide for the best trekking experience through the UNESCO Biosphere Reserve.
