Common Questions

Yes, walking the streets and ramparts of the Galle Fort is free. Individual museums and sites within the fort (such as the Maritime Museum) may charge a small entry fee of LKR 200–500.
The Galle Fort is approximately 1 km from the railway station — a pleasant 10–15 minute walk along the waterfront road. Tuk-tuks are also readily available and cost around LKR 100–200 for the short ride.
Unawatuna has a sheltered bay which generally makes it one of the safer beaches for swimming in the south. However, always check local conditions before entering the water, especially during the monsoon season (May–September when the southwest monsoon affects the south coast).
This website does not support ticket booking. Visit Galle is an information guide only. For train tickets, visit the Sri Lanka Railways website or purchase at the station. Bus tickets are typically purchased on board.
The best time to visit Galle is from December to April, during the dry season on the south coast. July and August also see calmer weather. The southwest monsoon (May–September) brings rain and rougher seas, though the fort and cultural sites remain accessible.
